Recommended Practices for Alpha Research
Dos

DO make sure your Alpha has economic foundation. A good practice would be to check if you could explain your Alpha to someone within a minute.
DO simplify the implementation of an Alpha idea and remove unnecessary elements: parts that do not make sense or do not drive performance.
DO assign meaningful names to the variables in your Alpha code to ensure readability.
DO follow theme in your Alpha research to create Alphas for higher payout.
DO use different datasets & operators to build Alphas to ensure diversification
DO search new/different/unique idea, from different papers, blogs, and articles. For example, papers on Quant research.
DO run simulations with different sub-universe (TOP 500/1000/2000/3000) while tuning the Alpha parameters. This reduces the risk of universe fitting.
DO restrict parameter search to simple & reasonable ones. For example 5, 20, 60, 120, 252 in case of days, instead of 37, 14 etc.
DO check your Alpha’s performance stability across sub-universe, super-universe and across other regions for higher OS performance.
DO improve Alphas by refining ideas, not by adding or fitting parameters, factors or reversion elements.
DO ensure coverage by backfilling data fields and the final Alpha using operators such as ts_backfill, group_backfill, group_extra.
DO use exit triggers (e.g. stop-loss) to close position while using trade_when operator.
DO normalize data fields to remove any firm related bias for cross-sectional comparison.
DO use automation for cutting down the redundant task in your Alpha making process
DO ensure that Alphas make financial sense after automation.
Don’ts

DO NOT spend too much time on a single simple idea.
DO NOT incorporate noise in the Alpha to reduce production correlation.
DO NOT add an existing Alpha from one data source to enhance the signal from another data source without any strong economic sense.
DO NOT over fit your Alpha to enhance performance or to reduce correlation by
Trying many different parameters or using too many data fields
Using too many if else
Shortening IS period
Filtering or presetting signals
DO NOT use automation as a source for generating Alpha ideas.
